<Section icon={Shield} title="Optimal Early Game Resource Allocation">
New players begin with a decent starting pool of resources, Black Books, and β chips. How you manage these initial assets will set the pace for your **Last Origin R resource exploration**. Blindly spending can lead to inefficiencies and slow down your progress.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Initial Resources">
    You start with approximately **300k resources**. Save these for offline recovery and equipment enhancement. Avoid dumping them into heavy construction early on, as it's inefficient.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Black Books Management">
    You receive **2 Black Books**. These instantly max a unit's level and skills. **Save them for late-game meta units** like No.85 Invincible Dragon. If absolutely necessary, use one on T-14 Miho or No.7 Scissors Lise to accelerate early farming.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Equipment Enhancement">
    Upgrade your free β chips (CRIT, ATK, EVA, SPD) to **+10** first. Equip CRIT/ATK chips on T-14 Miho for immediate impact on damage output during resource exploration.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Core Usage">
    Use **blue common cores** freely for early links. **Gold rare cores** are extremely limited; save them exclusively for top-tier endgame units such as No.2 Lavata and No.85 Invincible Dragon.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="info">
Efficient resource allocation from the start ensures a smoother progression and minimizes the need for excessive grinding later. Focus on enhancing units that contribute directly to your **Last Origin R resource exploration** teams.
</Callout>

By following these guidelines, you can ensure your initial resources are put to the best use, paving the way for a strong foundation in Last Origin R.

</Section>
