### Steps to Building an Effective Squad

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Assess Unit Strengths and Weaknesses">
    Begin by thoroughly reviewing each of your **last origin r units**. Understand their primary stats, active and passive skills, and their ideal combat range. Some units excel at close-range combat, while others are best deployed from a distance. Identify their unique contributions to a team.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Define Your Combat Strategy">
    Before assembling, decide on your general strategy. Are you aiming for an aggressive, offense-heavy approach, a defensive formation focused on attrition, or a balanced team? Your strategy will dictate the mix of Attackers, Defenders, and Supporters you need.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Prioritize Synergy and Buffs">
    Look for units that provide synergistic buffs or debuffs. Many units have skills that enhance allies within a certain range or debuff enemies. Stacking these effects can lead to exponential power gains. For example, an Attacker benefiting from multiple ATK buffs can become an unstoppable force.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Optimize Formation on the 9-Grid">
    Place your Defenders on the front lines to absorb damage. Position Attackers where they can safely unleash their skills, potentially behind Defenders or on flanks. Supporters should be in safe positions, typically the back row, where they can reach all allies with their buffs and heals. Ensure critical units are protected from immediate threats.
  </Step>
  <Step num="5" title="Train and Enhance Key Units">
    Once your core squad is established, prioritize training and enhancing your most valuable units. This includes leveling them up, upgrading their skills, and equipping them with the best gear. Consistent enhancement ensures your squad remains competitive against increasingly difficult challenges.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="warning" title="Avoid Over-Specialization">
While specializing in a certain unit type can be powerful, an over-specialized squad might struggle against diverse enemy compositions. Aim for a balanced team that can handle various threats. For instance, a purely offensive team might lack sustainability, while a purely defensive team might struggle with damage output.
</Callout>
