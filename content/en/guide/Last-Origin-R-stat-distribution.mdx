<Section icon={Swords} title="Stat Allocation for Damage Dealers (DPS)">
Damage dealers are the backbone of your offensive strategy, and their stat distribution is critical for maximizing their output. The primary goal is to ensure they consistently land critical hits and deal significant damage.

<Callout type="tip" title="DPS Stat Priority">
Always aim for **100% Critical Rate** first for your Damage Dealers, including all passives and equipment chips. Once this threshold is met, allocate **all remaining points into ATK** to maximize their damage per hit.
</Callout>

Here's a breakdown of key stats for DPS units:

| Stat             | Priority                                   | Description                                                     |
|------------------|--------------------------------------------|-----------------------------------------------------------------|
| **Critical Rate**| Highest (to 100%)                          | Ensures critical hits, significantly boosting damage.           |
| **ATK**          | Second (all remaining points)              | Directly increases the base damage of attacks.                  |
| Accuracy         | Important (300% threshold for endgame)     | Prevents attacks from missing, especially against high-evasion foes. |
| Armor Penetration| Important (90% threshold for endgame)      | Reduces enemy defense, allowing more damage to go through.      |

<Section icon={Shield} title="Stat Allocation for Tanks: Evasion and Defense">
Tanks are essential for protecting your damage dealers and controlling the battlefield. There are two main types of tanks, each with distinct stat allocation strategies.

<Callout type="warning" title="Tanking Mistakes">
Neglecting a tank's primary defensive stat (Evasion for Evasion Tanks, DEF/HP for Defense Tanks) will lead to them being quickly defeated, leaving your DPS vulnerable.
</Callout>

### Evasion Tanks

Evasion Tanks rely on dodging incoming attacks to survive. Their survivability is directly tied to their Evasion stat.

| Stat      | Priority      | Description                                                |
|-----------|---------------|------------------------------------------------------------|
| **Evasion** | Max Out       | Crucial for dodging attacks and remaining on the field.    |
| HP        | Secondary     | Provides a small buffer if an attack does connect.         |
| DEF       | Low           | Generally less effective than Evasion for these units.     |

**Example:** P-24 Pinto is an excellent early-game Evasion Tank. Focus all stat points on **Evasion** for her.

### Defense Tanks

Defense Tanks absorb damage through high Defense and Health Pool. They are designed to soak hits and protect the frontline.

| Stat      | Priority      | Description                                                |
|-----------|---------------|------------------------------------------------------------|
| **DEF**   | Balanced with HP | Reduces incoming physical damage.                          |
| **HP**    | Balanced with DEF | Increases the total health pool, allowing more hits to be taken. |
| Evasion   | Low           | Not their primary defensive mechanism.                     |

**Example:** AS-12 Steel Draco is a reliable Physical Defense Tank. Allocate points between **DEF and HP** to create a balanced, durable frontline.

</Section>

<Section icon={Trophy} title="Common Beginner Mistakes in Stat Distribution">
New players often make mistakes that can hinder their progress. Avoiding these pitfalls is crucial for efficient account building in Last Origin R.

<Callout type="warning" title="Avoid These Stat Distribution Traps">
- **Ignoring Stat Thresholds:** Failing to reach 100% Crit Rate, 300% Accuracy, or 90% Armor Penetration for DPS units will severely impact their performance, even with high ATK.
- **Random Allocation:** Blindly distributing points across multiple stats without understanding the unit's role or optimal thresholds is inefficient.
- **Wasting Black Books:** These valuable items instantly max a unit's level and skills. Save them for late-game meta units like **No.85 Invincible Dragon**. Using them on early-game units like T-14 Miho should only be considered if absolutely necessary for a quick push.
</Callout>
