<Section icon={Swords} title="Mastering 9-Grid Combat and Tactics">

Combat in Last Origin R+ is defined by the 9-grid battlefield. Unlike traditional turn-based RPGs where positioning is secondary, here it determines your survival. The grid is divided into Front, Middle, and Back rows. Units have specific attack ranges and area-of-effect (AoE) patterns that make placement the most critical part of your strategy.

| Unit Role | Primary Position | Main Objective |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Defender** | Front Row | Absorb damage/Protect allies |
| **Attacker** | Middle/Back Row | Deal high damage/Eliminate threats |
| **Supporter** | Back Row | Buff allies/Control turn order |
| **Tactician** | Flexible | Debuff enemies/Manipulate the grid |

<Callout type="success" title="Formation Bonus">
Always check unit passive skills. Many Bioroids provide buffs to adjacent cells. Placing a Supporter directly behind a Defender can significantly increase the team's overall durability.
</Callout>

The turn system is individual, meaning units act based on their Action Points (AP). High-speed units can often act twice before a slower enemy takes a single turn. Managing your AP is essential for timing defensive skills before an enemy boss unleashes a powerful AoE attack.

| Mechanic | Impact on Battle | Strategic Advice |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **AP (Action Points)** | Determines turn order | Use speed buffs to act first |
| **Grid Buffs** | Increases stats based on position | Align units to overlap buff zones |
| **Skill Range** | Limits which enemies you can hit | Move units to reach backline snipers |
| **Elemental Weakness** | Multiplies damage dealt | Match Bioroid types to enemy vulnerabilities |
