<Section icon={Target} title="Stat Allocation and Combat Mastery">

One of the biggest mistakes new players make is misallocating stat points. Unlike other RPGs where you might balance stats, **last origin r** rewards specialization. If a unit is meant to do damage, every point must go toward ensuring that damage lands and crits.

**Universal Stat Rules:**
- **For DPS Units**: You must reach **100% Critical Rate** first. This calculation includes passive skills, equipment (chips), and base stats. Once you hit 100%, dump all remaining points into **ATK**.
- **For Evasion Tanks**: Survival is binary. If you don't evade, you die. Maximize **Evasion** above all else.
- **For Defense Tanks**: Balance points between **DEF and HP** to ensure they can survive multiple physical strikes.

| Stat Type | Benchmark | Importance |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Critical Rate** | 100% | Ensures consistent damage and triggers certain passives. |
| **Accuracy** | 300% | Necessary to hit high-evasion enemies in later chapters. |
| **Armor Pen** | 90%+ | Essential for bypassing enemy damage reduction. |
| **Evasion** | Max | The only way for evasion tanks to function in the meta. |

<Callout type="info" title="Combat Modes">
The game features **Auto/Semi-Auto** and **Manual** modes. Beginners should stick to Auto-farming to build resources. Manual mode is only necessary for the hardest endgame content where skill timing and target priority are critical for survival.
</Callout>

<Section icon={Shield} title="Resource Management & Tuna Can Tips">

**Tuna Cans** are the premium currency in **last origin r**. While it might be tempting to spend them on beautiful skins for your Bioroids, doing so early on will severely hamper your progression. Account utility is far more valuable than aesthetics in the first few months of play.

**Priority Spending for Tuna Cans:**
1.  **Expanded Squad Deployment Bays**: You want to reach 5 squads as quickly as possible. This allows you to send more teams on resource explorations while you farm or sleep.
2.  **Character Slots**: As you farm, your inventory will fill up with units. Having more slots reduces the frequency of needing to clear inventory.
3.  **Equipment Slots**: Similar to character slots, you'll need space for the SS-tier gear you farm in EX stages.
